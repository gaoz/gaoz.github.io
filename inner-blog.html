Meet the Tour Guides of E Noa

by Dale

Posted on December 24, 2014 at 9:00 PM

One of the fun things about traveling is the interesting people you meet. They are the gateway to the hidden secrets of the fabulous places you might not otherwise hear about or the bits of trivia or really interesting historical knowledge that might not make the mainstream history books, because, well, maybe the author’s grandfather just wasn’t there...

And wouldn’t it be fabulous if you could get a whole bunch of these really interesting people together and persuade them to spend their lives sharing their wonderful knowledge (and personalities) so that all you wonderful Hawaii visitors could come spend the day with them?

Can you imagine how phenomenal these people would be if they spent 10 years doing this? What about 20 years? How about 30?

Are you beginning to get an idea of what an E Noa Tour Guide might be like?

They take you on a Pearl Harbor Tour – historians every one – but their parents and grandparents were here, so they have personal stories that make history just a little more real… Tour Guide Gaylen spent years as a volunteer guide at the Arizona Memorial, Tour Guide Bambi’s father was a soldier at Schofield Barracks when it was attacked during the Pearl harbor attack. And who better to give a tour of the Battleship Missouri than Tour Guide Pedro, who actually served aboard her?

They drive by Hawaiian Cultural sites all over the island on their Circle Island Tours – most of them are Hawaiian and have deep connections to these places – this history is their history. In fact Tour Guide Alex even wrote a book on Hawaiian History. And of course we even have our resident Hula Dancers – Tour Guides Kanilehua and Vanessa
